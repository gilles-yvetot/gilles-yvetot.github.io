Pilot Training TV Episode #15 – User Fees and CRM

by Jason Schappert | 4 comments

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+

This weeks episode of pilot training tv was a blast! We talked about user fees, crew resource management, and why the FAA is sending 24,000 pilots back to the books.

Text Transcript

This episode of Pilot Training TV is sponsored by Pilot Life, makers of the aviation lifestyle. Today’s episode talks about why 24,000 pilots had to go back and receive crew resource management training and see if it applies to you.

Also, we’ll be talking about aviation user fees and will they sneak into the FAA’s 2012 budget? I’m Jason Schappert and this Pilot Training TV live.

———

In today’s featured segment, 116 House of Representatives members signed a letter to President Barack Obama stating they’re all against aviation user fees to help fund the FAA’s 2012 budget. Now, this is definitely a step in the right direction in the fight on user fees especially given the big mix up in the elections in the House.

Now, AOPA President Greg Fuller believes that aviation user fees are actually a step backwards and could be detrimental to safety in the United States if we impose user fees. Now, to give you a quick rant about my personal opinion about it… and I want to hear what you guys have to say in the comment box below, especially if you guys are flying in a country that may already have aviation user fees. We would love to have your input.

I personally agree with AOPA President Greg Fuller. Aviation fees are definitely a step in the wrong direction. Think about what it would do to general aviation as a whole and as a business. Flight training is already suffering economically. We don’t need to add any more dampers and add any more cause to this.

Just look at what is happening out on California with all these imposed fees on flight schools and flight instructors. I personally had three California flight instructors call me up asking if I was hiring at my flight school here in Florida. The flight schools, or the flight instructors are leaving California because of these fees.

Imagine if we got a nationwide user fee imposed on us. It’d be absolutely detrimental to general aviation. I’m strongly against user fees. I’m a flight school owner, I’m a general aviation enthusiast and I’m a really dedicated flight instructor. So maybe I have a little bit of a bias, but I’m strongly against user fees.

I want general aviation to thrive and prosper, and we need a future of pilots. There has to be a future to take over these airliners, these Part 135 operations. So what’s going to happen?
I wanna know what you guys think about user fees. Why are you for them or why you are against them. Scroll down underneath this box and go ahead and leave me a comment in the chat. If you’re watching a recorded version of this a little bit later after the live show, it’s gonna be on MzeroA.com… scroll down and leave me a comment on MzeroA.com.

I wannna hear what you guys think about user fees on general aviation.

————-

Our Ask Jason question this week comes from Desmond in Atlanta, Georgia. Desmond asked:

“Jason, you’ve really been pushing and talking about CRM or crew resource management. I already have my private pilot certificate and I wannt to know how I can become a better crew member when flying with friends on local flights.”

Well, Desmond, excellent question. This brings me to a great point. Actually, the FAA just released a statement saying 24,000 Part 135 or chartered pilots are gonna be going back and will be doing some CRM training.

It’s been coming up on checkride after checkride. “What is CRM?” It’s usually the question that’s asked, and now the FAA is even retraining chartered pilots on the use of Crew Resource Management so this is a huge topic right now.

For you, as a private pilot, whether you’re flying with a friend or even your wife or a non-pilot, think about it like this. You can use them for real basic tasks. Let’s use the non-pilot scenario. You can simply ask them, “Hey, will you help me look for other traffic?” or “Hey, right now, we’re looking for this link that’s shaped like this. It’s our next checkpoint. Why don’t you help me keep an eye out for that?”

If you’re lucky enough to have another pilot on board, work as the flying pilot and the non-flying pilot. Have the non-flying pilot work on the radio. Writing down frequencies, helping you navigate, while you focus on what’s important and that is aviating. That is, flying the airplane.

So there are a lot of avenues to go. Just because someone you’re flying with isn’t a pilot, it doesn’t mean you don’t have to include them, especially if you’re in an uncontrolled airport on a Saturday morning. You know how busy that could get. Use that non-pilot to your advantage and use them to help you.

———–

Let’s go ahead and around the pattern in 60 seconds.

A professor at MIT released his research saying that there wasn’t a single fatality on a Part 121 or a scheduled airliner in a first world country in 2010. Now this is great news but keep in mind that this is only applying to the first world countries. Perhaps it’s good news to those that have friends and family that are nervous about flying on aircraft.

In Vero Beach, Florida, Piper announces its discontinuing and dropping its Piper Sport LSA stating a difference in business philosophy. This of course is in reference to its Czech Republic supplier of the Piper Sport aircraft.

And the FAA also just recently released its statement saying that there are 2,836 laser incidents in 2010. We’re talking about shining a laser at an aircraft. This is double from the 1,527 in 2009. The FAA also wants to make clear that it is up to 20 years in prison and a $250,000 fine for shining a laser at an aircraft.

That is your around the pattern in 60 seconds.

————

Hey, I hope you guys really enjoyed today’s episode of Pilot Training TV Live. I see your questions coming in there in the comment box and chat box, so I’m gonna go ahead and take some special time after the show to get to those.

Thank you so much for everything. If you guys are on MzeroA.com watching the recorded version of this, scroll down, leave me a comment below what you thought about the show, what you think about some of the topics in it especially talking about user fees.

That’s all I have for you guys today. Most importantly guys, remember, a good pilot is always learning. Have a great day guys see you later.
