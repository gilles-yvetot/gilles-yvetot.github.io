]]> By: Mark C. http://www.m0a.com/vor-navigation-made-easy/comment-page-1/#comment-6583 Thu, 01 Dec 2011 17:08:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3147#comment-6583 Well, the initial objective was to fly to the VOR on a heading of 090. Although as I originally commented, that’s not a good real-world example, if you wanted to fly to the station, most likely you’d tune the station, determine the most direct route, and fly that heading – in the video, I’d guess that to be about 030 from the initial position, which would make you inbound on the 210 radial, but you’d still set the OBS to 030, as that’s the heading you want to fly to the station, and by selecting that bearing all you have to do is fly toward the needle to return to course if you get off.  I’m not saying your method is wrong, just for those who may not be as familiar w/VOR nav, they need to be aware of the behavior of the indicator needle as it relates to where you are and where you set the OBS. To go back to the 270/090 scenario – you are west of the VOR and want to fly toward it on a heading of 090. If you set the OBS to 270 the needle will indicate to the right, but you must fly to the left to intercept the course. If you set the OBS to 090, the needle will indicate to the left and you must fly to the left – much easier for me, especially under the hood, in actual IMC, or at night, which is when I primarily use VOR nav – fly toward the needle. The nav system makes the calculations for you – it knows that the radial is actually the 270/090 radial, depending on your direction of travel, and east of the VOR, it’s the 090/270 radial.

]]> By: Mark C. http://www.m0a.com/vor-navigation-made-easy/comment-page-1/#comment-6581 Thu, 01 Dec 2011 15:46:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3147#comment-6581 Tracy,

You do not want to select the 270 radial to fly to the station if you’re west of the VOR. Doing so would result in exactly what you don’t want – a “reverse” situation. In the example, if you set the OBS to 270 and flew the 360 to intercept, as you turned to 060 or whatever to intercept the 090 course, the needle would be to the right and if you “chased” the needle as you should when using a VOR, it would lead you off course to the south. By selecting 090, as you turn to 060, the needle will indicate to the left and will gradually center as you approach the proper course to the station, keeping you flying on a NE heading which you’d gradually shallow to 090 as the needle showed you were approaching your course.
