]]> By: Steve http://www.m0a.com/airplane-stall/comment-page-1/#comment-4390 Sat, 13 Nov 2010 13:22:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3101#comment-4390 Jason,

Excellent explanation of the subject. Years back, I was very nervous about stalls, and as it was years of no flying since my previous bi-annual review, some of those old fears have tried to reassert themselves. I did some aerobatic training to combat my fear be making the airplane do these unusual behaviors, so I could grow accustomed to what the airplane would do. I made the instructor teach me stalls, and we went up in an Aerobat. I was very nervous about an uncoordinated stall, because my first instructor always was very alarmed about my not being exactly coordinated in the stall. So we went up and spun that Aerobat until I was satisfied. The C150 is very hard to spin, and you have to really want to, or it just won’t do it. the most I could get it to do was 1.5 to 2 spins at the most- then it would come out all on its own- even with me trying to hold it in. But the number one thing that alleviated my fear was not doing the stalls themselves, but in flying paper airplanes and rubber powered model airplanes. They naturally go up, stall, dip down and continue flying. So I think of that, and let the airplane do the same while I am flying. And, I try to focus my mind on ‘feeling’ what the airplane is doing. Tune into the airplane, and it will not surprise you. After all, it wants to fly.
