Abbreviation for condensation trail. When a jet engine takes air in, it is compressed and ignited. The hot air, in excess of 600 degrees Celsius, rushes out the back and comes into contact with the outside air which is about -56 degrees Celsius. If there is sufficient moisture in the air, as the air cools it condenses into a “cloud.” This is mostly Ice crystals up his high, but they look like clouds to you and I from the ground. They naturally “move” based upon the upper air flow, ie “jet stream.”
