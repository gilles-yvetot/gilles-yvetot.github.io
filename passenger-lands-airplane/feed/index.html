]]> By: Dave http://www.m0a.com/passenger-lands-airplane/comment-page-1/#comment-4956 Sat, 19 Feb 2011 02:43:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3285#comment-4956 I thought the guy did a great job on such short notice. I’m very familiar with this transcript. I too am a low time pilot. For me, I would have probably gotten control of the airplane first. I f you can’t control the AP, the only solution is to shut the AP down, get her straight and level and hand fly it. (Basic Pilot Skills) I then would have declared an emergency and and started hunting for the POH which should be within reach and started to get a general idea of my V-speeds. Sometimes they are printed or stamped in plain view on the panel.

I too am a low time pilot but when I board ANY aircraft, I’m not just a passenger; I’m a pilot. And I don’t mean a bold pilot. This may seem silly to some pilots but before I board any aircraft even if it’s a commercial airliner, I do a little research on that aircraft and I even print out a copy of the v speeds for that bird and stick them in my pocket. Yes, many aircraft today have some very complex systems but I am not easily intimidated by them. It’s an aircraft. I can get around the systems especially with some help on the phone or radio. What I want to know is how much we weigh, what are the the limitations of that aircraft and how much fuel is on board. Hopefully I won’t ever get this opportunity and the cost of another persons life but I am always asking myself the question that we all should be asking ourselves; WHAT IF??

One thing I learned in my early years ago hanging around corporate pilots- what separates the professionals from the private fliers, is not the ability to fly, but to do it precisely, and to be ready for any circumstance, and have a plan to get out of it.
