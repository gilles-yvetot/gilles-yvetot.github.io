Ok so this one is a bit different from your traditional short field landing. Not only is it a short field but it’s also a soft field. So the technique is going to be a little different. You’re going to really enjoy this video.

Well what did you think? Leave me a comment below I’d love to hear your opinion or story!

How do we combine all those maneuvers? So let’s go ahead and jump to the footage. We’ll start when I’m on the downwind with the landing, and then we’ll go ahead and show you guys the takeoff.

Nice descent down. Alright, bringing it all out here. That’s cool. Verifying final’s clear. Maybe I should go 20 degrees flaps, it’s a little bit hot here.

Bringing it on in here. Airspeed’s looking great. Here it comes. I’m going to hold this nose off, I’ve gotta protect this nose gear as long as possible.

So, cool, I will talk with you guys when we’re heading back out for the takeoff, because the takeoff will gonna be even more interesting. I’ll catch you guys in a second.

Alright, so we’re getting ready to take off from our little 2200 feet strip, all grass, short. You saw the landing. Let me go ahead and show you the take off. The important thing here is we want to keep the airplane rolling. There’s some reasoning behind this. It’s gonna be hard to come in on a dead stop and get that engine going on a soft field using a short field or a typical short field takeoff. You’re gonna come to a dead stop. I’ll show you guys a little tip and trick, so let’s go ahead and rock and roll here.

My reasoning behind this is: it’s short and it’s soft field. I want to build up some momentum to power me through that. It’ll come in off the taxiway. Unlike a typical soft field landing where you’re sitting there, holding the brakes, you’re revving them up.
