by Jason Schappert | 63 comments

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+

So with last weeks post on What Makes A Great Pilot You guys really came out in numbers and showed your opinion. On top of that some of the most valuable content for new and emerging pilots was shared there so a HUGE thank you!

I won’t spoil the video… But one of the posts brought up talking about flight instructors. Which made me ask myself “Well, what really does make a great flight instructor?

Check out the video then as always leave me a comment below.

What do you think makes a great flight instructor? Tell me below!

Text Transcript

Hey everyone, Jason Schappert here of MzeroA.com.

Last week, I posted a really great question. “What makes a great pilot?” I got a ton of responses to this. One of you guys said, Avoid complacency. Always learning, which is the real principle we really teach here at MzeroA.com.

But one person said something that really sparked my interest. They said, a great pilot is made by a great flight instructor. And I really agree with that because a flight instructor is responsible for giving that good base.

In the US, the pilot (after you pass your checkride, after soloing during your training) is responsible for going out and continuing your education because a good pilot really is always learning.

But I really believe that the flight instructor is there to do that solid base to help you build good study habits and have it all come together and make sense.

So now I put the question today, “What do you think makes a great flight instructor?”

Now, again, I want you leave your comment at MzeroA.com underneath this video. But I may give you my piece of advice for this, what my opinion is for this. Maybe you agree and maybe you don’t agree. But one thing I find and one thing I think really makes a great flight instructor is, when looking for a flight instructor, you need to find one that is full time.

I know that in these tough economic times it’s hard to find anybody that’s full time at something now. Lots of people have different side jobs. They’re doing different things. Now, how can you be full time and fully devoted to your training if you have an instructor that is only part-time devoted to you?

So, you really need to think about that. I’m not saying part time instructors are bad. If you just wanna fly on the weekends, a part-time instructor can make it done. However, if you wanna fly two or three times per week and really knock it up, and really get the most bang for you buck, save money on your training and knock it all up, be a safe, great pilot… I really recommend finding a full time flight instructor.

That is just my opinion, maybe you agree, maybe you disagree.

But I wanna hear what you guys think. What qualities make a great flight instructor. Is it personality? Is it knowledge? What qualities of character… what makes a great flight instructor. Go ahead and go to MzeroA.com if you’re not already there. Scroll down beneath this video and leave me a comment. You don’t have to register or anything like that; just go ahead and type your comment in there and you know you’ll get a response from me.

I really appreciate that guys. I can’t wait to see what you guys have to say. And most importantly, remember, a good pilot is always learning. Have a great day, guys. See ya.

  • Mike

    Jason, I respect your opinions but I must disagree with the insinuation that full-time instructors are automatically better. In getting my certificate I had 5 instructors, 3 are ‘full-time’, 2 are ‘part-time in that they hold other non-aviation jobs… all with the same FBO. Even the 2 ‘full-timers’ weren’t full-time instructors, they spent as much time flying charters, etc. as they did instructing. All were different in personality, methods, etc. but 4 were good and 1 was lousy in my opinion. Of the 2 best ones, 1 was full-time and 1 was part-time. The lousy one was full-time!

    From what I’ve read and heard from many others, there are more poor instructors out there than good ones. For what it’s worth, from what I know about you I think you’re probably one of the good ones.

    Good ones are outstanding pilots themselves, they have excellent judgement, they are mature in attitude (and I don’t necessarily mean age as mine ranged from 23 to 50-something). They have the students best interest at heart and it’s not just superficial. They don’t milk the students for more hours. They know how to teach and ‘handle’ various student personalities. They have empathy. They don’t hesitate to get on a student, hard if necessary, when needed… but they also don’t hesitate to shower a student with praise when justified. They try hard to keep a student’s enthusiasm up, and don’t kick a student when he/she is down. They treat you as a friend as well as a customer, yet know how to draw the line. They challenge a student, yet don’t overload a student. They make learning to fly FUN, not a drudgery. I could go on & on.

    Students for their private certificates are new at this, it’s a leap of faith for them, and an expensive one at that. They start out putting all their faith in their instructor, completely trusting him or her. They just don’t know any better at this point. They don’t know, don’t realize if they’ve got a good, bad, or so-so instructor until well into it with many dollars spent because they just don’t know. Sometimes they don’t even know until well after the fact, after the checkride, if their wallet has been taken for a ride. Too often it’s only after the fact, in hindsight after they’ve been in/around aviation a while that they come to realize there’s often a big difference in instructors.

    Just because a person has a CFI rating doesn’t automatically mean they are good instructors…. and the same goes for full-time.
