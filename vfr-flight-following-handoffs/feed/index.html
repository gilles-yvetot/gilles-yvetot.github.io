However, a VFR FP is like kissing your sister. It never gets to a controller. (Most CFIs don’t know this). The FSS keeps it in a drawer in case you come up missing. That’s why, if your really go down, you ARE going to spend the night on the ground (unless you crash very close to civilization). It’s much more practical, and useful to ask for flight following. An IFR FP is handled differently, and in fact goes to the controllers. However, they NEVER see your filed alternate. Again, that’s like kissing your sister. It’s just there to make you go through the fuel planning exercise and to give you an initial choose when things start to go sour.

]]> By: Thomas http://www.m0a.com/vfr-flight-following-handoffs/comment-page-1/#comment-5377 Tue, 10 May 2011 20:47:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3388#comment-5377 The video was good. I don’t say as much as you did though. When being
handed off from one controller to another, I repeat back the frequency, n
number and add “good day / thank you”. On the new frequency, I listen
for a couple of seconds and say airspace(eg Norcal), n number, altitude,
and if I am level, climbing, or descending(most of the time I’m level).

On a side note, the ATC folks in my area are GREAT. All but one
conversation, I’ve had has been positive with many ATC folks making sure
I’m aware of traffic. It is just awesome to have another person helping
keep me safe; it’s how a pilot can utilize ALL of his/her resources.

It also seems to help if you make the call in a nice steady even tone of
voice and not too fast similar to what you demonstrated.

Don’t forget, everyone can always ask the departing controller to
“repeat” or “say again” to confirm one has the right frequency to change
over to. Plus, the sectional sometimes has the new frequency printed
right there for you to read.

My instructor always wants me to know where I am while flying and to
watch the GPS, sectional and most of all scan the outside airspace where
you are going to fly next. I can hear him saying, “don’t get
complacent” what are you going to need / do next?

Thanks for posting this as it reminds me to review past experiences and keep on learning.
