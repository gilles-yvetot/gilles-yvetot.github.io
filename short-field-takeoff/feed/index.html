]]> By: Kenn3621 http://www.m0a.com/short-field-takeoff/comment-page-1/#comment-7313 Tue, 03 Jul 2012 02:32:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3851#comment-7313 Short  field in a Piper.  Lineup, put on parking brake, get your feet off the brakes(this prevents inadvertant brake drag from not getting completely off the brakes), full power, check instruments and begin T/O roll, as speed increases gradually pull back on yoke as aircraft feels light pull in first notch of flaps and climb at Vx till clear of obstacle, verify positive rate of climb take out flaps and climb at Vy.  This is not by the book but it reduces the ground run a significient amount.

]]> By: Matthew Everett http://www.m0a.com/short-field-takeoff/comment-page-1/#comment-7302 Mon, 02 Jul 2012 22:31:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3851#comment-7302 A piece of advice I received for a true short field situation is to mark the midpoint of the runway (or whatever you’re using as one) with something you can clearly see from the cockpit. Then, on takeoff, if you’ve not reached 75% of your takeoff speed by that point, stop. The idea being, you don’t want to run off the end and that way you’ve got the same amount of runway available for stopping as you had to reach whatever speed you were going.

Note: you must still always do the performance calculations.
