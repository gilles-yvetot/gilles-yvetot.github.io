Well I did with pen and paper.. First and third was accurate and the second was more of approximate.

here is how I calculated..:

Q1: Very simple 85 kts for 35 minutes how much is distance travelled?

Ans: We know that 85 kts for 60 minutes will travel 85 nm

so in 35 minutes will be 85 / 60 x 35 = 49.5 nm

Q2: not so easy True course 170, wind 220 at 12, airplane speed 110 kts find Ground speed

Ans: Here a little approximation factor comes. If the aircraft was flying directly into wind it would have been easy 110 – 12 = 98. but there is 50 degrees variation. So I did an approximation of around 3 and calculated the speed to be 101.

But this was approximation

Q3: Again easy to calculate.: climbing to 4500 at 500 fps speed 70kts calculate distance.

Ans. First calculate the time to reach 4500: climbing at 500 feet per minute. which is 4500 / 500 = 9 minutes. Then calculate the distance traveled in 9 minutes traveling at 70 kts. which is again easy like in first question:
in 60 min distance traveled is 70 nm
so in 9 min the distance should be 70 / 60 x 9 = 10.5 nm which is the answer.
