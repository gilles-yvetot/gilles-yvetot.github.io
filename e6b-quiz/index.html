E6B Quiz

by Jason Schappert | 8 comments

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+

So how good are you at the ole’ wiz wheel?

Learning how to use a manual flight computer is valuable knowledge. Because as we all know electronics can sometimes fail us. I even know DPE’s (checkride examiners) who won’t even let students use anything EXCEPT a manual e6b flight computer. Test your skills in this e6b quiz where tomorrow i’ll share with you the answers and how I got them.



The Questions:

1. Your groundspeed is constant at 85kts. You’ve been flying for 35 minutes… How far have you travelled?

2. Your true course is 170 the wind is 220 at 12 your airplane cruises at 110kt. What is your groundspeed?

3. You’re leaving a sea level airport and climbing to 4,500ft your ground speed is 70kts you’re climbing at 500fpm. How far out from the airport should you expect to reach your top of climb?

One other thing I do is on question #2 for the wind and groundspeed I do this: I put my grommet (hole) on 100 and draw a line with my pencil or fine point washable crayola marker from the hole up and at the wind speed draw a little line. It looks like an airplane. Then I twist based on the true course. You then put the where the line meets the wing on your true airspeed and the grommet (hole) will be right over your GROUNDSPEED.   • Prabhjot Singh

    Thanks for the guide to use flight computer

    Well I did with pen and paper.. First and third was accurate and the second was more of approximate.

    here is how I calculated..:

    Q1: Very simple 85 kts for 35 minutes how much is distance travelled?

    Ans: We know that 85 kts for 60 minutes will travel 85 nm

    so in 35 minutes will be 85 / 60 x 35 = 49.5 nm

    Q2: not so easy True course 170, wind 220 at 12, airplane speed 110 kts find Ground speed

    Ans: Here a little approximation factor comes. If the aircraft was flying directly into wind it would have been easy 110 – 12 = 98. but there is 50 degrees variation. So I did an approximation of around 3 and calculated the speed to be 101.

    But this was approximation

    Q3: Again easy to calculate.: climbing to 4500 at 500 fps speed 70kts calculate distance.

    Ans. First calculate the time to reach 4500: climbing at 500 feet per minute. which is 4500 / 500 = 9 minutes. Then calculate the distance traveled in 9 minutes traveling at 70 kts. which is again easy like in first question:
    in 60 min distance traveled is 70 nm
    so in 9 min the distance should be 70 / 60 x 9 = 10.5 nm which is the answer.
