I teach my students to have a mental checklist and flow pattern and then go to the written checklist, the done list. So that equals 3 lists. The written checklist is my final check because as human beings we can get distracted by passengers, other pilots, tower control etc. The written checklist makes sure you have not forgotten anything. From my IMSAFE checklist, to my walking into the hanger big picture checklist, to my preflight checklist, to my CIGARS checklist, to my post takeoff checklist, to my cruise checklist to my pre-landing checklist, to my CGUMPS or GUMPS checklist to my off the runway checklist, to my shut down checklist. They all make sure I or my students do not miss anything that is important to the flight. I am an older dude and I remember a Peanuts comic strip that said, and I am paraphrasing, “I have never made a mistake in my life, I thought I did once, but I was wrong.”
