]]> By: Mark Newton http://www.m0a.com/inflight-engine-failure/comment-page-1/#comment-6209 Tue, 27 Sep 2011 07:57:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3458#comment-6209 One thing you didn’t cover which is pretty important during practicing is to think about safe landing options during the climb-out after you’ve completed the exercise.  One of the times when an engine is most likely to give out would be shortly after application of full power after a cooling descent.

In the video you picked a clear spot in the middle of a wooded area;  if you powered away and the engine quit right as you were crossing the upwind boundary of the safe landing area, would you be in a position where you were low, at or below climb speed, and with nothing but forest in front of you?

Something to think about for practice sessions.  In a real engine-out situation it’s obviously not important (!)
