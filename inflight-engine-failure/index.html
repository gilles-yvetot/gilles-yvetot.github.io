Inflight Engine Failure

by Jason Schappert | 28 comments

It’s something we train so much for. Yet it’s so unpredictable and the situations vary. However as pilots it’s so important to prepare ourselves for each possible situation. In this video I’ll share with you guys how I practice simulated engine failures.

  • Mark Newton

    One thing you didn’t cover which is pretty important during practicing is to think about safe landing options during the climb-out after you’ve completed the exercise.  One of the times when an engine is most likely to give out would be shortly after application of full power after a cooling descent.

    In the video you picked a clear spot in the middle of a wooded area;  if you powered away and the engine quit right as you were crossing the upwind boundary of the safe landing area, would you be in a position where you were low, at or below climb speed, and with nothing but forest in front of you?

    Something to think about for practice sessions.  In a real engine-out situation it’s obviously not important (!)
