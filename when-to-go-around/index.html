When To Go Around

by Jason Schappert | 21 comments

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+

We as pilots hate practicing go arounds! I mean why do I have to do it? Well a go around is without a doubt the most under-practiced flight maneuver. Below I’ll show you a great go around procedure and some reasons why you would do a go around.



What are some reasons you’ve done a go around? Tell me in the comment box below.

  • Larry M. Diamond

    This is incredible timing and so important. I love you Man. Here is my story from yesterday. Temperature 91 degrees, humidity 40%, variable winds, 5pm and I am taking a private pilot in a DA20 to a 2500 foot grass strip about 20 miles east of Ann Arbor, MI. Due to the density altitude, thermals, 75 foot trees at the threshold, my fantastic Instrument student had to do 3 go-arounds because the Diamond did not want to land. Each go around was during the landing flare. The 90% mental, I would change to 95%. When flying into areas that require pretty tight tolerances the go-around should be a high priority item on final approach. This video is going on my private pilot syllabus. 

  • Mike King

    Soon after getting my Private Ticket. I took a friend up and around the state. When I fly back to my home airport (towered), I was cleared for landing on runway 32R, I noticed that a Bonanza off to my 9 o’clock position who was given clearence for runway 32L  sped up and shot accross me to land on the incorrect runway. I was in a continuous position to land on my assigned runway but obviously the other Pilot got confused. He landed ahead of me and I quickly called Tower and CCCC’ed it came around in the pattern and made a greasy landing on Runway 32R. Thank goodness for Situational Awareness. 

  • WilliamRobinson2006

    I felt like quitting flying after my 2nd attempted landing on my 1st solo after having to do a go around with little experience practicing them close to the ground.

    I forgot to anticipat right rudder and was 10 feet off the ground over the grass off the side of the runway. I forgot to anticipate forward pressure and didn’t have enough lift to take off again and was slowly sinking. And I forgot to stop the flaps at 20 degrees and when they went to 0 degrees the plane sank a couple feet and my life flashed before me. Luckily I was able to regain control and it taught me a good lesson.

    Practice practice practice go arounds both in the air and close to the ground.

  • Gkouba

    I go around when small corrections in aimpoint, airspeed, and glide path are not working. Over controlling the airplane in landing configuration low to the ground is not the time to fight. Also wild life like birds and deer and maybe people, or a runway incursion by another aircraft. and such on the runway. Just go around. And remember full throttle, RIGHT RUDDER!,  hold that yoke ( fight the bloat) and be aware of airspeed, get one notch of flaps up ( if full) and climb at  VX. Fly the plane. Positive rate of climb start to retract remaining flaps. Then make your radio call when able.

Another aircraft in the pattern witnessed the event and estimated we were less than 6-8 seconds from making contact with one another.

    I’ve gone around many times, but that one will be memorable for awhile…
