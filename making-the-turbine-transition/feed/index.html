I was originally taught to fly angle of attack on approach Â in anticipation that I would go to the airlines and fly jets. This was not my request, but how they taught me. Problem is, you really should not fly angle of attack in a piston, which I am flying. I recently got some additional training as I prepare for the instrument check ride, and this was the first thing my instructor wanted to undo. He has 15,000 hours with the airlines, and he said I need to fly how the airplane I am in needs to fly, not like some jet I might fly in the future. It took a couple hours before I could see the difference since it was so ingrained in me. He does own an L-39, so I might get a chance in the future to fly a jet but for the mean time, I will focus on the 172 at hand. The real lesson- treat them all differently, because they are all very different. Don’t just learn to fly, learn to fly that specific airplane, and don’t expect other airplanes the handle the same.
