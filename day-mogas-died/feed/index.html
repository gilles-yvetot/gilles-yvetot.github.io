Well, one day when the wind died and we were three miles from shore on Lake Michigan, I reached over for my lengthy excerise session at the stern of the boat (starting the engine before I installed a remote starter).  Cursing the fact I had to use the ‘iron sail’, I did in fact however breath a sigh of relief when it started. 

It lasted 10 minutes before we it quit and we had a small fire.  From my pilot training I’m always safety conscious and constantly develop back up plans and I had the fire extinguisher working in seconds flat.  

After a LENGTHY row back to our dock (more contingency plan results — have an oar!), we found that the hose that fed the carb had a big honkin’ hole in it.  A mechanic then asked me where I got my fuel.  

The rest is history.  10% Ethenol = 100% future failure and expense.
