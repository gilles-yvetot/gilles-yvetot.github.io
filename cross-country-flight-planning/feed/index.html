]]> By: Bob Barbanes http://www.m0a.com/cross-country-flight-planning/comment-page-1/#comment-4131 Sat, 09 Oct 2010 01:10:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3019#comment-4131 Another awesome and relavent video, Jason! You know what? I’m a pretty high-time pilot, but I fly a low-level helicopter. Believe it or not, when we have a flight to a destination we haven’t been to before, I still draw a line on a map with a highlighter. I look for any airports I might fly over, prominent landmarks, SUA, or other features (e.g. really tall antennas!) that might interfere with my flight. I don’t make 10-mile tick-marks anymore, but I do measure the distance and mark the halfway point. (Plus, it’s fun to see what stuff I’m going to fly over!) My advice: Use BIG landmarks, not little ones that may be hard to see from altitude.

It would be very easy…too easy, actually to just rely on the GPS to do this flight planning for me. But I don’t like surprises. My idea of a perfect flight is one in which everything went “pretty much” according to plan and I didn’t have any surprises of the bad kind.

Lay the charts out, then draw the line. Even some of us pros still do it.
