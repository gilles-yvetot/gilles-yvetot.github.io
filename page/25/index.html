Crosswind Landings

Jun 20, 2013

I know crosswind landings are TOUGH! TO share a quick story with you… It wasn’t until halfway through my commercial pilot certificate that they finally one day clicked! I know plently of people in this position, Private Pilots who aren’t totally confident when it comes to landing in a crosswind. We have too many opportunities to shy away from them. (There is almost always a runway favoring the wind at big airports) However that won’t always be the case. Rather than shying away from crosswinds (like I did for so long) let’s learn more about them and start little by little building up not only our skill but our confidence. Short Field Landings

Jun 17, 2013

Its one thing to practice short field landings (in preparation for our private pilot checkride) on a beautiful 6,000x100ft paved runway in a Cessna 150. It’s another thing to have a real short field landing on your hands. Short/narrow strip… A classic “50ft obstacle” and an airplane that can easily make the landing… assuming you (the pilot in command) actually fly it by the book (the Pilots Operating Handbook) I had just that opportunity the other day flying with an MzeroA fan and friend Darren. Darren owns and flies a BEAUTIFUL turbo charged Commander and is a regular at short field landings. So when I asked him to fly into the Albert Whitted airport (roughly 3,000ft of usable runway with multiple obstacles on the approach) It was no sweat for him. The Pilot’s Operating handbook for the Commander told us we only needed 2,200ft of runway over a 50ft obstacle that day.
