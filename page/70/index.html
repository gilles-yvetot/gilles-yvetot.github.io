Are the training curriculum any different etc. etc. We’re going to answer all your questions once and for all below. Still have a question? Leave me a comment under the video. Text Transcript Hey everyone. Jason Schappert of MzeroA.com, bringing to you more Pilot Training TV. I get tons of emails per week. One of the most popular emails I get is, “Hey Jason. I’m getting ready to learn to fly. Should I learn to fly Part 61 or Part 141?” I’ve read a little bit about the two, but I’m really not sure.” So, I got a great answer for that partly because I did half my training Part 61 and the other half Part 141. Let me explain the differences between the two. Part 61 is… we’ll call it more like your mom and pop operation. When I teach my students to fly, I teach them Part 61. It’s just me and an airplane. We go up, we fly, we meet all the Part 61 requirements, we go up for your checkride. We kinda dictate what happens that day. Versus Part 141 has a structured syllabus. Hey, on this day, we’re gonna do steep turns and stalls and short field landings. They have a syllabus. Each and every day you know what you’re gonna do.
