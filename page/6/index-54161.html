3 Flight Maneuvers You Need To Master

by Jason Schappert

In your flight training you go over dozens of maneuvers. Some seen mundane while others challenge every bit of your flying ability. Regardless if you’re learning to fly or a seasoned pilot below are 3 flight maneuvers that you should practice frequently and master every aspect. Unusual Attitudes “But Jason unusual attitudes make me sick, can we go easy this time?” That was a response from a student when I asked him to set us up for unusual attitudes. I know they can be hard on your stomach but you’ll build a tolerance. Flying under the hood, especially experiencing spatial disorientation is key in flying. When you can master unusual attitudes you’ll make yourself a safer pilot. Emergency Procedures Engine failure in flight, engine fire on start, electrical fire in flight, just to name a few. Go through your airplanes checklist and know each emergencies procedures inside and out. Learn them on the ground and simulate them in the air. Preparing for the unexpected is a sure fire way to succeed in aviation. Stalls Now don’t get confused here, in fact remember this saying “We practice stalls to practice recoveries. Not to practice how to get into a stall.” That’s the keyword here “recovery” do you know how to recover from a power on stall? Turning stall? Accelerated stall? Learn these procedures and their appropriate recoveries. You never know when you’ll need them. Above is just 3, the list could go on and on!
