The C-130 was coast guard so he was doing practice with some new pilots most likely. (Thus the touch and go's)

So you're correct you've now got wake turbulence no matter which way you choose.

Here's the breakdown on wake time limits by ATC

If YOU ask for your wake seperation time ATC must give it to you. However THEY are NOT required to offer it. (silly I know, so pilots must educated themselves and know to ask for it) Now that's not saying a kind controller won't offer, some do..

Taking off behind a heavy aircraft 3 mins
Landing behind a heavy aircraft 2 mins

Wake vorticites tend to descend at a rate of 300 to 500fpm for the first 30 seconds then spread out and downward at a much slower rate as they begin to dissipate.

So 3 and 2 mins are pretty safe

]]> By: Koehn http://www.m0a.com/strange-question/comment-page-1/#comment-3386 Wed, 28 Jul 2010 23:17:55 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=2648#comment-3386 Since wake turbulence descends at roughly five hundred feet per minute, waiting someplace is the thing to do. You don't want to be doing your 360 in a place where the wake turbulence might be blown by the prevailing wind, so if there's a right crosswind do your 360 on the right side of the field. That way the turbulence will be blown to the left and away from your aircraft.
