]]> By: Jason Burke http://www.m0a.com/flying-in-a-thunderstorm/comment-page-1/#comment-4193 Thu, 21 Oct 2010 03:39:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3052#comment-4193 Interesting question, and interesting responses so far. As a low-time VFR pilot, I would have to agree completely with your assessment. As one who would rather be old than bold, and given our knowledge of these storms’ ferocity, it would be a poor choice indeed to push through. But…

As an avid reader of aviation history, I was actually surprised that you didn’t have more disagreement with your statement. I suspect there may be a demographic issue that may be illustrating a major shift in pilot risk management and ADM. Long story short, reading about the exploits of pilots from the early days (take Earnest Gann for example), there are frequent references to horrible turbulence, thunderstorms, icing, and all manner of death-defying (and some not-so-defying) adventures.

Could it simply be that many of the books written with the “push through” mentality reflect the attitudes and technology of that era? Even those written in the last 20 years still might have those sorts of pilots as an audience, or at least those sorts of pilots as instructors of said audience, for better or worse. Perhaps your site, attracting (I’ll guess) a younger and perhaps more conservative breed of pilots, reflects the changing attitudes that come with learning from others’ mistakes.

Keep up the great work!
