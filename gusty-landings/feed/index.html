]]> By: Larry M. Diamond http://www.m0a.com/gusty-landings/comment-page-1/#comment-6106 Sun, 18 Sep 2011 16:17:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3464#comment-6106 First a comment and then a story. Did the student first crab and do a side slip? I teach to side slip all the way down because it gives the pilot a chance to figure how much  aileron to use for drift and then how much rudder to keep the longitudinal axis of the airplane down the center line(I call this pilot muscle memory). I know gusty conditions make this little harder. I feel the “kick out” method happens way too fast for an inexperience pilot to be able to correct for the gusts and not put side loads on the aircraft. Flew in 1987(way before you were born) from KDTW in a Piper Warrior going to Jackson MI(KJXN). Door popped open over KARB winds 240 at 20 knots and landed. Runway at KARB 24/06. Had breakfast. Took off from KARB winds 260 at 18 Knots. Called DTW tower winds 270 at 25 knots gusting to 45 knots. Wind shear alert in effect, Boeing 747 on final reported losing 15 knots. Tower asked if I wanted to declare an emergency replied negative. On final to Runway 23 Center had no flaps in, carrying 80 knot airspeed, airplane heading 270 tracking 230 down on final. Did a kick out with full right aileron full left rudder and did a squeaker on the centerline. Said a prayer to Mr. Piper and taxied to the hanger.
