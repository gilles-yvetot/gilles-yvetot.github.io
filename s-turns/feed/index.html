Diamond http://www.m0a.com/s-turns/comment-page-1/#comment-6663 Sat, 17 Dec 2011 21:19:00 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=3552#comment-6663 Since it is 26 degrees and snowing here I feel the Ocala warmth already. I teach S-turns based on 2 words: Winds and Groundspeed. If the winds are coming from behind you faster groundspeed steeper turn. Into the wind, slower groundspeed, shallower bank. I have the student always enter on the downwind and before they cross the road I will ask and remind them “where are winds and what is your groundspeed” (fast or slow). Since it is downwind and the groundspeed is the fastest the first turn to the left will be the steepest and I want them to paint half circles over the top of the road. As they continue the circle back to the road the groundspeed will be slower into the wind and the bank will be shallower. Now is the really exciting part as they cross the road going into the wind, I have them go into a very shallow bank and say 1001, 1002, 1003 and then start a little bigger bank. This keeps the circle nice and round and keep them from going into too steep of a bank and flattening their circle like a pancake. Happy Holidays Jason. Strong work as always.
