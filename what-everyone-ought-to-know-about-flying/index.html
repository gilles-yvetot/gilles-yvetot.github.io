  • Mike W.

    myself, I teach every student the 3-2-1 rule. Its easy, you end up at tpa exactly if you use this formula. you take your altitude and subtract it from the tpa, exampe youre at 5,000 feet and tpa is 1,000. thats 4,000 feet you have to descend. you divide your ground speed by 2 and add a zero (ex 100kts is 500) that gives you your fpm rate of descent to maintain a 1,000 ft descent for every 3 sm. and you start that descent at 12 miles since that is 3 x the change in altitude, in this case being 4. works like a charm!!!
