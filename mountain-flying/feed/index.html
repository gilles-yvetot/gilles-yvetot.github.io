We pull crashed airplanes off the tops every year. The major cause of accidents? NOT density altitude. The primary cause of accidents in the Smokies is VFR into IMC which ends in controlled flight into terrain. Also, strong winds are a major factor too but usually are in conjunction with IMC. Add to this that there are far more airplanes in the Southeast that have to cross the Appalachians to get somewhere, one could argue that the Appalachians can be far more dangerous than the Rockies due to population.
