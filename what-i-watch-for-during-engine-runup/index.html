by Jason Schappert | 29 comments

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+

In todays quick video I not only want to show you a proper engine runup inside my cessna 150 but also some things I watch for. For example what am I really watching for on my mag drop? What would it mean if I had low suction? High or low oil pressure? etc…

Check out the video below then leave me a comment under the video on some things you watch for during your engine runup.

  • Tom

    A couple added tips that I’ll add:

    – When checking mags, do the right one (2 clicks over) first, then finish with the left for on click back to both. You’ll NEVER end up taking off on one mag accidentally (done that!)

    – Checking carb heat, let it sit for maybe 10 seconds. It can take some time for ice to melt, a brief check may not be long enough. (at least here in soggy, cool Seattle!).

    Great videos!

    Tom – CFI RNT/BFI

  • Larry M Diamond

    Here is my routine for all airplanes especially the Cessna 152. 1) circuit breakers in. I have caught a couple of times that one circuit breaker popped out during taxi. 2) I always point the plane into the wind for max cooling for the runup. 3)For controls I do “thumb up aileron up” If you turn the yoke to the right for instance your top hand thumb will point to the up aileron. A little silly but it works. 4) as stated below when the carb heat comes out, look for a drop then the throttle goes to idle. The same as in the transition to landing setting.5) Lights, Camera, Action. Landing light on within 10 miles of the airport for other airplanes to see me. Camera – transponder on ALT and 1200. Just to make sure the students are paying attention I will change one of the zero’s to a 2 to see if they are paying attention. I had DPE’s try this on checkrides for me and I have caught it everytime. 6) My passenger briefing is ABCCDE A- AIRPLANES all passengers and pilot looking for aircraft, B- Belts how to get in and out of the seatbelts, C – do not touch the CONTROLS unless I say too. C – sterile COCKPIT for the first 5 minutes until out of the traffic area, D – doors
    Larry “Biggest Mzero Fan” Diamond

  • Rich

    An instructor of mine had a good recommendation years ago that I always follow now. I turn the fuel selector switch to the lower of my tanks on initial starting. I then run on that tank while taxiing from the parking/tie down area to the run up area. Then when I begin my run up I switch to the other tank (fullest), finish the run up and take off with this tank. This insures you’ve run the engine from fuel in both tanks before take off. Good idea in case you have water or bad gas in a tank.

  • mzeroa

    I like your idea of taxi on one…. then switch…runup and leave it on that tank. As you know it works
