]]> By: Femtoman http://www.m0a.com/aviation-radio-your-first-solo/comment-page-1/#comment-3096 Tue, 11 May 2010 23:22:22 +0000 http://www.m0a.com/?p=2445#comment-3096 What a coincidence, I flew my first solo this morning as well! I did four full-stop landings. I was pretty nervous on my first two landings and my base and final honestly were pretty bad – I had to use 40 degrees flaps on the first one since I came in too high, and I agree completely, it felt like the Cessna 150 turned into a giant parachute. I also tended to gain quite a bit of altitude in the pattern without the instructor, but I adjusted and it went fine. The last two landings were nice and soft. I've found on landings, the approach is just as important as the roundout and flare. A bad base leg and final tend to throw off the rest of the landing due to the increased number of variables that have to be monitored and corrected. All in all it was a lot of fun and I'm looking forward to now building up solo hours and moving onto cross country flights!
