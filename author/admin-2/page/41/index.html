Uncontrolled Airport Operations

by Jason Schappert

Here’s a great chance for you to tag along with me on a routine flight with one of my students. Listen as he makes he radio calls, handles traffic conflicts, and brings the airplane in for landing. Uncontrolled airport radio communications come easy for some and more difficult for others. What’s your experience? Let me hear it below. A few of my tips for SAFER uncontrolled airport operations: Tip 1 Make your radio calls while you’re turning or banking it’s much easier to see a turning aircraft then one that’s level. Example: Call you crosswind as soon as you start the turn from the upwind. Tip 2 Remember just because you’re making your radio calls doesn’t mean everyone else is. It’s not required to say A WORD at an uncontrolled field so stay vigilant. Tip 3 Start and end every radio call with what airport you are at! Example: Dunnellon Traffic ….. and end with Dunnellon Traffic. I can’t tell you how many times I or my student asked “where is he?” because the pilot failed to mention his airport twice. What’s your experience with uncontrolled airports? I’d love to hear! Leave me a comment...
