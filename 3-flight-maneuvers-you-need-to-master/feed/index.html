I was surprised by this request since instructors have always have always had me put my head down and they had their rare chance to actually fly the plane.  Flying with my eyes closed to deliberately put the plane in an unusual attitude after basic instrument maneuvers enforced that I need to consider cues from the airplane such as the sound of the wind related to airspeed.  After 20 seconds or so, the DPE told me to open my eyes and recover.

The entire setup and recovery was valuable to me since I was flying the plane with no ground or instrument references.  The DPE was excellent, not only because I passed the practical test, and helped me consider more options once I received my Private Pilot Certificate.
